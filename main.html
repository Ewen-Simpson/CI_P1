What is a fox?

A fox is any member of the the vulpes genus of the sub-family caninae, making them closely related to wolves, jackals, coyotes and even domestic dogs.

Fake foxes?

Not all animals that are called foxes are actually true foxes. An example of this would be the crab-eating fox which belongs to the genus cerdocyon and the various sub-species of South American foxes which belong to the genus lycalopex

How do foxes communicate?

Foxes are known to make a number of different vocalisations to communicate, as well as making use of scent markings like domestic dogs as well as a basic form of body language.
